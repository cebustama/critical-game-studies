# Research Index — Critical Game Studies

## Completed Research Threads

### 1. Materialist Analysis of Videogame History
- **Status:** COMPLETE (v2 — deepened)
- **Documents:** materialist-analysis-videogames.md, materialist-analysis-deepened.md
- **Key frameworks:** Gramsci hegemony, M-C-M' accumulation loop, procedural rhetoric (Bogost), capitalist realism (Fisher), platform capitalism (Srnicek), cynical ideology (Žižek)
- **Key findings:**
  - Mechanics encode ideology through enactment, not depiction — the player *performs* capitalist logic
  - Accumulation loops (kill → loot → level → kill harder) reproduce the circuit of capital
  - The tech tree encodes Whig history as game mechanic — teleological, universal, convergent on Western modernity
  - Scarcity and abundance are dialectically related in game design, not simply opposed
  - Player agency is significantly undertheorized in ideological critique — players are active producers of meaning, not blank ideological surfaces (Stuart Hall's encoding/decoding model)
  - Crunch is both structural exploitation and genuine creative experience — the contradiction is politically productive
  - The arcade was simultaneously disciplinary apparatus and site of working-class access
- **Gaps identified:** gender/sexuality, disability, non-Western critical traditions (Fanon, Quijano, Mignolo), phenomenology of play, ecological crisis as frame, affect theory (Ahmed, Berlant)

### 2. Colonialism in Game Systems
- **Status:** COMPLETE (deepened with material history)
- **Documents:** materialist-analysis-videogames.md (Part III), materialist-analysis-deepened.md (Section III)
- **Key findings:**
  - Civilization's mechanical framework structurally excludes indigenous political ontology (Cree case study: Milton Tootoosis critique)
  - Hardware supply chain (coltan/cobalt in DRC) constitutes material colonialism enabling digital play
  - Indigenous game design (Never Alone, Sámi Game Jam) is a different *practice*, not just different content
  - Latin American game culture developed through piracy as informal distribution infrastructure — modding as cultural sovereignty
  - South Korea's esports emerged from 1997 financial crisis, not from natural "evolution" of gaming
  - China's state-capital nexus (Tencent, CCP regulation) challenges Western frameworks assuming state/market opposition
- **Gaps identified:** deeper engagement with Quijano's coloniality of power, more non-Western game examples, Mignolo's epistemic delinking

### 3. Non-Colonial Game Design
- **Status:** COMPLETE (design document)
- **Document:** knotlands-design-document.md
- **Key systems designed:**
  - Ecological metabolism (dynamic stocks/flows with nonlinear thresholds)
  - Asymmetric knowledge systems (five cultures perceiving different layers of same simulation)
  - Cyclical temporality (seasonal + generational, no tech tree)
  - Obligation economy (gift exchange, no universal currency)
  - Collective deliberation mechanic (player as participant, not sovereign)
  - Violence as costly last resort (ecological damage, intergenerational trauma, relational destruction)
- **Self-critique included:** player sovereignty problem, designed-system problem, representation ≠ sovereignty, market remains

## Cross-Cutting Analytical Framework

- **Document:** materialist-analysis-deepened.md (Section VII — Revised Analytical Framework)
- **Five layers:**
  1. Material Archaeology (production, distribution, consumption conditions)
  2. Systemic Grammar (verbs, nouns, syntax of the game's mechanics)
  3. Ideological Operation (what does the player learn to take for granted?)
  4. Contradictions and Excess (where does the game undermine its own ideology?)
  5. Positionality of Analyst (who is performing this analysis, from where?)
- **Status:** COMPLETE, applicable to any new research thread
- **Note:** When starting a new thread, consider whether this framework needs adaptation for the specific topic or whether it can be applied directly.
