## What Has Been Done

### Analytical Work
- **Materialist analysis of videogame history** — complete, two-document set (~15,000 words). Covers arcade era through live service, mechanics as ideology, colonialism in game systems, capitalism simulation, hegemony and player subjectivity, counterexamples, and design implications.
- **Deepened analysis** — addresses simplifications in the original, adds contradictions (arcade as both disciplinary and accessible, crunch as both exploitative and creative), Global South perspectives (South Korea, Latin America, China), material colonial history (coltan, cobalt, Cree critique of Civ VI), and converts design provocations into actionable mechanical specifications.
- **Revised analytical framework** — five-layer method applicable to any game: Material Archaeology → Systemic Grammar → Ideological Operation → Contradictions & Excess → Positionality of Analyst.

### Design Work
- **Knotlands** — complete game design document for a non-colonial strategy game. Five asymmetric cultures, ecological metabolism, cyclical time, obligation economy, collective deliberation, violence as costly last resort. Includes honest self-critique.
- **PROGRESS** — complete game poem design and working React prototype. 24-tap extraction game with permanent death via persistent storage. Designed for Game Poems Magazine.

- Martin (2023): military recruitment through popular media — military-entertainment complex
- Noriega v. Activision Blizzard: legal case on historical figures in games

## Open Questions Requiring Research or Discussion

1. **Althusser vs Gramsci:** Seiwald uses Althusser's ISA framework. The materialist analysis uses Gramsci's hegemony. These are related but distinct — Althusser emphasizes structural reproduction through apparatuses; Gramsci emphasizes consent-production and the possibility of counter-hegemony. How should these be integrated? Are they complementary tools for different aspects of the same phenomenon?

2. **Framework adaptation:** Can the five-layer analytical framework be applied directly to propaganda analysis and representation analysis, or does it need modification? The "Systemic Grammar" layer works well for mechanics-as-ideology but may need expansion for narrative/representational analysis.

3. **Non-Western scholarship:** The analysis repeatedly identifies the absence of non-Western critical frameworks as a limitation. What specific scholars and traditions should be prioritized? Fanon (colonial psychology), Quijano (coloniality of power), Mignolo (epistemic delinking), Rivera Cusicanqui (ch'ixi methodology), Mbembe (necropolitics) are named but not yet engaged.

4. **Design practice vs design product:** The Knotlands document and the deepened analysis both distinguish between making games with different *content* and making games through different *practices* (cooperatives, indigenous collaboration, community design). This distinction needs more development — it's currently more asserted than demonstrated.
